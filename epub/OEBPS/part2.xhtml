PART 2

Jesus vs. Paul

In Part 1, we examined the fundamental divide between Yahweh—the deity of the Old Testament who demanded sacrifice, executed judgment, and commanded violence—and Jesus' Father, who revealed Himself through mercy, compassion, and unconditional love. We saw how Jesus shattered Yahweh's Law, rejected the sacrificial system, and exposed a God whose very nature contradicts the portrait painted in much of the Hebrew scriptures.

But this divide didn't simply fade away after Jesus' death. Instead, it was preserved—and even amplified—through a theological hijacking that would shape Christianity for two millennia. Now we turn to the man who became Christianity's most influential voice: Saul of Tarsus, later known as Paul. His vision on the Damascus Road didn't just convert a persecutor—it gave birth to a gospel that would reinterpret Jesus' life, death, and message in ways that fundamentally clash with the teachings of the man who walked in flesh among us.
