PART 3

Bible Texts Altered

In Part 2, we witnessed how Paul's theological vision—born on the Damascus Road—reinterpreted Jesus' life and message, transforming a teacher of mercy into a blood sacrifice and shifting the focus from obedience to belief. We saw how Paul's writings, amplified by letters forged in his name, became the dominant voice in early Christianity, effectively hijacking Jesus' legacy.

In Chapter 8, we saw how both theological reinterpretation and potential textual insertions worked hand in hand—how passages like Matthew 5:17-19 may have been inserted to make it appear Jesus endorsed Yahweh's Law, even while his actions throughout his life flatly contradicted those words. We saw how scribal insertions like the "unpardonable sin" were used to control and threaten those who questioned emerging orthodoxies.

But this was only the beginning. The physical alteration of texts was far more extensive than isolated insertions. Scribes—both accidentally and intentionally—modified, inserted, and removed passages to support emerging doctrines, reinforce power structures, and align scripture with theological agendas. In Part 3, we examine the full evidence: the manuscript alterations that helped preserve Yahweh's violent image, reinforced sacrificial theology, and silenced Jesus' radical message of mercy.
